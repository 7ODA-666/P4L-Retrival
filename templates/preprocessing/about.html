Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of breaking down text into individual words or phrases called tokens.

These tokens are the basic building blocks used in information retrieval.

Input: "Hello World!"
Tokens: ["Hello", "World", "!"]

Stop Words Removal

Stop words are common words (such as "the", "is", "at", "and", "to") that carry little meaning in retrieval tasks and are often removed to reduce indexing size and improve search efficiency.

Input: ["This", "is", "a", "test"]
Filtered: ["This", "test"]

Normalization

converting variations of the same word into a single representation.

This includes:

Case folding

Converting all words to lowercase.

Example: "AI Research" → "ai research"

Removing special characters

Strip punctuation and symbols that add noise but not meaning.

Example: "Hello, world!!!" → "Hello world"

Expanding contractions

Replace contractions with their full forms to improve consistency.

Example: "We're" → "we are"

Stemming

Stemming removes suffixes to obtain the base form of a word. It follows algorithmic rules and does not consider word meaning
(e.g., "ponies" -> "poni").

Input: ["running", "runs", "runner"]
Stemmed: ["run", "run", "runner"]

Lemmatization

Lemmatization converts words to their dictionary base form (lemma), considering the context and meaning of the word
(e.g., "better" -> "good").

Input: ["better", "running", "mice"]
Lemmas: ["good", "run", "mouse"]
