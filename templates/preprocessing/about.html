Tokenization

Tokenization is the process of breaking text into smaller pieces called tokens. These are typically words, subwords, or symbols. It's the foundational step for any NLP pipeline, as it defines the basic units of meaning the system will analyze.

Input: "Hello World!"
Tokens: ["Hello", "World", "!"]

Stop Words Removal

Stop words are extremely common words (like "the", "is", "at", "which") that carry very little useful information for searching or analysis. Removing them helps the system focus on the keywords that actually convey the core meaning.

Input: ["This", "is", "a", "test"]
Filtered: ["This", "test"]

Normalization

Normalization transforms text into a single canonical form to reduce variability and noise. Here are three common normalization operations with examples:

Case folding

Convert text to a single case so that variants match consistently.

Example: "AI Research" → "ai research"

Removing special characters

Strip punctuation and symbols that add noise but not meaning.

Example: "Hello, world!!!" → "Hello world"

Expanding contractions

Replace contractions with their full forms to improve consistency.

Example: "We're" → "we are"

Stemming

Stemming is a crude heuristic process that chops off the ends of words in the hope of achieving a root form. It operates very quickly by applying generalized suffix rules, though it sometimes produces non-real words (e.g., "ponies" -> "poni").

Input: ["running", "runs", "runner"]
Stemmed: ["run", "run", "runner"]

Lemmatization

Lemmatization considers the context and converts the word to its meaningful dictionary base form, called a lemma. While slower than stemming, it relies on linguistic rules and vocabularies to produce accurate, real words (e.g., "better" -> "good").

Input: ["better", "running", "mice"]
Lemmas: ["good", "run", "mouse"]
